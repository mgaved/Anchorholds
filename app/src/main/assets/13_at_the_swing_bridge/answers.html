Grammar

Practice: adverbials

  1. Correct: ‘on Sunday’ is a time adverb in the end position.
  2. Incorrect: ‘usually’ should be before the main verb (mid position) or at the end of the sentence. Correct: I usually take the 10:15 from Milton Keynes to London Euston.
  3. Correct: the adverb ‘kindly’ is before the main verb.
  4. Incorrect: ‘yesterday’ should be before the main verb (mid position) or at the end of the sentence. Correct: I went to London with Maria yesterday.
  5. Correct: Adverb ‘annoyingly’ is at the beginning of the sentence, expressing a point of view.
  6. Incorrect: ‘before the departure time’ should be at the end of the sentence. ‘Always’ is correct because it is before the main verb (mid position). Correct: You should always be ready to leave before the departure time.
  7. Incorrect: adverb should be at the end of the sentence. Correct: Are you returning the same day?

Language tips

Stress and meaning

Words that are important to meaning are stressed.

  • Main verbs: think, live, get off, get
  • Nouns: Bletchley, form, £30, card, discount
  • Adjectives / adverbs: fine, straight away

Negative auxiliaries are also stressed: e.g. ‘don’t, aren’t, can’t’.

Grammatical words are unstressed.

  • Pronouns: I, that, we
  • Prepositions: near, for
  • Articles: the
  • Auxiliary verbs: will
