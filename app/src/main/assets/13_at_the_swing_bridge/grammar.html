Grammar

Word order: adverbs

Use this exercise to remind you where adverbs or adverbial phrases go in a sentence.

There are three normal positions for an adverb.

1. At the beginning

Adverbs expressing points of view:

Unfortunately, there is a replacement bus service between Bletchley and Milton Keynes.

Luckily, I got a discount with my railcard.

Time adverbs can come here, especially when we want to emphasise them.

Yesterday, the trains weren’t running on time.

2. In the middle (before the main verb)

Adverbs expressing frequency:

I normally buy open returns because I don’t know exactly when I’m coming back.

I’ve never travelled in first class before.

Adverbs expressing certainty or probability:

I will probably go to London next week.

You have certainly saved some money by buying a railcard.

3. At the end

Time adverbs:

Coming back the same day?

Do I get the discount straight away?

Adverbs of place:

I will see you there.

We were waiting on the platform.
