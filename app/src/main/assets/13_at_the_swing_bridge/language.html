Language tips

Sentence stress

In English, the time between stressed words is the same. This gives English a rhythm.

Listen and repeat these sentences from the dialogue. Pay attention to the stressed words and the rhythm of the sentences.

I’d like to get to London on Friday please.


All you need to do is fill out this form.


You can make significant savings if you’re a regular traveller.

Notice how unstressed words are made shorter or are said quickly. This keeps the time between the stressed words the same.

Stress and meaning

In English, we put stress on the words that are most important to the meaning of the sentence.

Listen to this section of Erica’s story. Think about the words that are stressed and unstressed. What do you notice? Are they all important words?

Erica: Oh I see. I think that’ll be fine though. I live near Bletchley so we’ll get off there. Thanks. Here is the form. And £30 for the card. Do I get the discount straight away?

Question: what categories (kinds of) words are stressed or unstressed here?

Answers

Listen and repeat these sentences. Make sure that you put the main stress on the most important words.

Does it make a difference?


She’s two years old.


So it would be smart to get a railcard now.

Contrast

When we speak, we can use stress to show contrast (difference) between two things.

Listen and repeat the sentences below. Notice how the words we want to contrast have extra stress because they are most important.

And I can come back on any train? I don’t have to book a specific train?


Day return is £15.50. Open return is £22.00.

Grammatical words like pronouns (you, she, we etc.) are normally unstressed but they can have the main stress if they become more important. Here, you and she are stressed to show contrast.

Listen and repeat.

I’m afraid you won’t. But she will.
